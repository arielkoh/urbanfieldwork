Code and Data

Code and Data looks at different types of data and graphs. A numeric data set is formatted first and then displayed through code. Three types of graphs are addressed here: Line, Bar and Donut graph.

3 Graphs

Graphs are great visual tools to illustrate data. When done well, information can be relayed comprehensively and effectively, while the visual aspect creates an opportunity to create an aesthetic that might be able to convey messages such as branding.

Line

A line chart is a type of chart that displays information using data points called “markers” connected by straight line segments. It is similar to the spline graph, but the spline graph draws a curved line between the points instead of straight lines. The line graph comprises two axes known as the “x” axis and “y” axis. A bar graph also known as bar chart is a graphical display of data using bars of different heights.

Bar

Bar graphs can either be horizontal or vertical, depending on the variable of graphing. There are a few types of variables–nominal, ordinal, interval and ratio variables. Horizontal bar graphs are preferred when graphing nominal (or categorical) variables, while vertical bar graphs are preferred when graphing ordinal (or sequential) variables.

Donut

Donut graphs are pie charts with a blank centre, and are used to represent data that are part of a whole 100%. It focuses on the proportions of the varying data within the entire sample size. There may be off-sets to this sort of data-representation, compared to a line or bar graph, as the human eye might not be as quick to register data angled in a circle rather than seeing the differences between heights of, for example, the bar graph. However, the fact that all the data adds up to 100% is an important and useful factor––perhaps useful in contexts such as representing the different races in an entire population (100%).

Your Data Graph

With interest in cryptocurrency trading, our group decided to create a market chart for Ethereum(ETH) for the year 2021. Aimed to focus on ether price trends, we excluded actual values, to bring more attention to the periods of highs and lows throughout the year. To create this graph, a total of 236 data points were plotted, which required us to stretch the graph to limit clutter, link to old line graph.

The line graph didn’t provide enough information for users. With the intention of showing ether price trends, The graph was improved with labels and axes created by using Adobe Illustrator. The values of the highest peak and lowest dip was added as well as an x-axis showing the months of the year for each value.

The graph was completed by adding margins around it, and labelling the y-axis. Below you can find the data that we have used for our line graph.

Data Interpretation

In May 2021, Ethereum prices hit a new all time high of $4167.78 as shown in the graph. Our group was curious as to what affected the change in price and went on to research for possible causes. One reason in particular, caught our eyes. According to Fortune magazine, experts mention that Ethereum prices benefit from increased transactions and a spike in NFT (non-fungible tokens) purchases on the Ethereum blockchain. (Kastrenakes) This sparked an idea to pixelate the line graph created in p5js, imitating the look of Beeple’s NFT, which sold for $69 million.
